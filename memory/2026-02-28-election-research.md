## WILKES BOARD OF EDUCATION (Non-partisan - 2 Seats)

### Rudy Holbrook (Incumbent)
- **Background:** Chairman of Wilkes County Board of Education, businessman with 46+ years experience
- **Education:** Not specified in sources
- **Experience:** Current Board Chairman, extensive business background dealing with people daily
- **Platform:** 
  - Student safety as top priority
  - Prepare students for post-graduation careers (college or workforce)
  - Address school budget challenges
  - Help every student successfully finish school
- **Why Running:** "I want the kids to always be safe. I want to help prepare all of our kids for whatever career that they want after they graduate from high school."
- **Key Issues:** School budget challenges, student success

### Jammie Jolly (Incumbent)
- **Background:** Product of Wilkes County Schools, former teacher, instructional coach, mom, wife, Christian
- **Education:** Teaching degree from Appalachian State University; Master's degree from East Carolina University
- **Experience:** 
  - 32.4 years in public education
  - 18 years as classroom teacher
  - 10 years as instructional specialist in Wilkes County Schools
  - 4 years as Director of School Improvement for Winston-Salem/Forsyth County Schools
  - First term on Wilkes County Board of Education
  - Co-owner of Jolly Bell's Education Consultants
- **Platform:**
  - Steady, thoughtful leadership
  - Experience and consistency matter
  - Strong stewardship of taxpayer dollars
  - Data-informed decisions instead of across-the-board cuts
  - Strengthen partnerships with county leaders, community organizations, businesses
  - Advocate for fair, sustainable funding for rural districts
- **Key Issues:** Declining enrollment combined with rising operational costs, budget planning, protecting instructional quality

### Jose Rivera (Challenger)
- **Background:** Doctor, semi-retired physician and educator, pastor
- **Education:** Not specified; taught from middle school through graduate university levels
- **Experience:** 
  - Past educator (middle school through graduate level)
  - 14 years as board member of South Carolina Minority Affairs Commission
  - Experience engaging lawmakers for educational change
- **Platform:**
  - Enable students to fulfill God-given talent in learning
  - Encourage teachers and students to find areas of aptitude
  - Address curriculum issues - teachers feel held back from actual teaching
  - Focus on passing tests vs. gaining life knowledge
- **Campaign Themes:** Faith, Family, Education
- **Why Running:** Alarmed by students not knowing current events or post-graduation plans; called to intervene by "Lord and Savior, Jesus Christ"
- **Key Issues:** Funding, curriculum, social issues, family engagement with teachers

## WILKES COUNTY SHERIFF (Republican Primary - 1 Seat, 6 Candidates)

### Chris Shew (Incumbent)
- **Background:** Lifelong Wilkes County resident, career law enforcement since 1980
- **Experience:** 
  - Sheriff since 2010 (serving 4th term, seeking 5th term)
  - Served under 3 sheriffs and 2 chiefs of police
  - Worked through every rank: Deputy Sheriff, Patrol Sergeant, Patrol Captain, Detective Captain, Chief of Detectives, Major, Chief Deputy
  - Chief Detective 1986-2001
  - Chief Deputy 2001-2010
  - NC Lottery Commissioner for 7 years (Vice Chair, Operations/Personnel Committee Chair)
  - NC Sheriffs' Association Legislative Committee
  - Roaring River Fire Department Board of Directors
- **Certifications:**
  - Sheriff's Training and Standards Commission certified
  - Sheriff's Leadership Institute graduate (UNC Chapel Hill)
  - Criminal Justice Commission certification
  - Advanced Law Enforcement Certificate
  - Advanced Service Award
- **Key Accomplishments:**
  - Implemented FLOCK Automated License Plate Readers
  - Established Medical Assisted Treatment (MAT) program in jail for substance abuse
  - Launched Law Enforcement Assisted Diversion (LEAD) program for drug use, poverty, mental health challenges
- **Platform:**
  - Continue aggressive drug enforcement
  - Address emerging threats: cybercrime, cryptocurrency offenses, civil unrest, mental illness challenges
  - Focus on supporting deputies so they can serve citizens
- **Key Issues:** Drugs, cybercrime, mental health, officer support
